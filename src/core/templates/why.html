Why This Project

Helping agencies prevent dangerous culvert failures with clear, data-driven ratings.

Why Culverts Matter for Road Safety

Culverts are essential for moving water under roads and protecting the pavement above. When a culvert deteriorates or collapses, the effects can be sudden and severe: lane closures, washouts, and unsafe conditions for drivers. Even a single failure can disrupt traffic, damage infrastructure, and require costly, emergency repairs.

This project is motivated by the need to reduce those disasters. Instead of waiting for visible distress or a collapse, agencies need a practical way to spot culverts that are more likely to be in poor condition and prioritize them for inspection or intervention.

Purpose of the Platform

The Culvert Prediction Predictor is designed to give transportation agencies, municipal engineers, and researchers a fast, consistent way to estimate a culvert’s condition on a simple 1–5 scale using a small set of inputs such as material, shape, age, soil pH, etc.

With given input, our system returns:

  • A predicted 1–5 rating for the culvert’s overall condition.
  • A list of the different models outputs to show any discrepancies

The goal is to give engineers a smarter starting for discussing risk, maintenance, and replacement priorities.

Impact on Public Safety

When a culvert collapses, it can undermine the road above it, force unexpected closures, and put drivers, pedestrians, and first responders in danger. By highlighting higher-risk candidates sooner, this platform helps agencies move from reactive repairs after a failure to proactive maintenance before the road is compromised.

The combination of:

  • A standardized 1–5 rating scale,
  • Outputs from different models

makes it easier to explain decisions to supervisors, district managers, and the public.

Cost-Effective Inspection and Maintenance Planning

Agencies often manage thousands of culverts with limited crews and budgets. Fixed-cycle inspections treat every structure the same, even though some are far more likely to be in poor condition than others. This tool supports risk-aware scheduling by pointing engineers toward the culverts most likely to need attention first.

Because the platform is lightweight and browser-based, planners can:

  • Run quick ratings in the field or at the office using data they already have.
  • Compare “what-if” scenarios when considering materials or replacement options.
  • Export PDFs to support budget requests, planning memos, or design reports.

Who This Helps

The platform is built for:

  • Field engineers who need quick, defensible ratings during site visits, along with PDFs they can attach to inspection notes.
  • District planners who need to screen many culverts and prioritize follow-up inspections, repairs, or replacements.
  • Consultants and researchers who want transparent, repeatable ratings to support memos, studies, and design recommendations.

Across all of these roles, the goal is the same: fewer surprises, fewer dangerous failures, and safer roads for the public.
